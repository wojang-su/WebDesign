Behavioral Therapy

What is Behavioral Therapy?

“When rewards don’t work, negative consequences of losing points may be necessary” Solanto M.,(2017).

Moreover, behaviors are easily influence in young ages, therefore, if parents learn how to interact with their children who has ADHD, then the children are able to succeed in building relationships.

However, the behavioral therapy does not solve core symptoms, it only shows children how to use skills to control the symptoms.

There are two approaches in the behavioral therapy. One is for the attention problem and another is for the behavior problem.

The approaches for attention problems focus to teach ADHD children new techniques, so they can focus on their work and manage their responsibilities at home.

The solutions for children who go to elementary school are checklists, time limit, using a planner, and rewards chart.
The approaches for behavior problems focus to help children’s impulsive behavior. This type of children creates conflict with family members at home and gets themselves into troubles at work. Both approached require parent training. The approaches focus on the interaction between children and parents.
